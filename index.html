THE COSMIC TIMELINE
T = 0

The Big Bang

13.8 billion years ago, everything began from a singularity of infinite density — an explosion that created space, time, matter, and energy itself.

380,000 years later

First Stars Ignite

The universe cools enough for hydrogen atoms to form. Gravity pulls them together into massive clouds that ignite into the very first stars — blazing beacons in an otherwise dark cosmos.

4.6 billion years ago

Our Solar System Forms

A nebula collapses under gravity, forming the Sun at its center. Rocky debris and gas coalesce into planets — Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, and the giants beyond.

3.8 billion years ago

Life on Earth

In the ancient oceans, the first self-replicating molecules emerge. Over billions of years, life diversifies — from single cells to the vast tapestry of species that cover our planet today.

10,000 years ago

Human Civilization

Humans settle, farm, and build. Cities rise from the dust. Empires emerge, collapse, and leave behind art, architecture, and ideas that echo across millennia.

1969 — present

The Internet Age

Humanity weaves a global neural network, linking billions of minds. Information flows at the speed of light, and the boundaries between the physical and digital worlds dissolve.

Right now

You Are Here

13.8 billion years of cosmic evolution led to this exact moment. You — a collection of stardust, carbon, and consciousness — reading these words at the edge of all we know.
